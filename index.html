About Me

Welcome!

I still remember the day my dad took me to the bank to exchange his Dutch Gulden for the equivalent in Euro's. And then a few months later when my mom handed me a 5 dollar American bill to buy a McDonald's Happy Meal. Man, those were the days...

Ever since I was a young age, I've been fascinated by new cultures, countries, and economies. When I was only 6 months old I moved from Den Bosch, the Netherlands to Rome, Italy, and then to Ilkey, England, and then to Vught, the Netherlands, and then to New Providence, New Jersey. Growing up my accent changed more times than my haircut!

I did my bachelor studies in the USA at Villanova University, where I pursued a double major in finance and international business. Although I spent all 4 years of college in the USA, I took the opportunity to study abroad in the summers! I spent a summer living in an ancient neighborhood in Rome, and another one living with a French "familie d'acceuil," attending the local French university to take French classes.

After graduating from college in the USA, my desire to learn more about the world sparked my decision to move from New Providence, New Jersey to Amsterdam, the Netherlands to pursue an M.S. in International Management. It was during that wonderful year in Amsterdam that I learned all about how companies compete in the global marketplace, enter new markets, and overcome the challenges to cross-cultural business. I even got to do an internship at a multi-national company headquartered a 15 minute bike-ride from where I was living: Kraft-Heinz.



It was at Kraft-Heinz where I discovered another passion of mine: Supply Chain Management. I was fascinated by how massive corporations could, at scale, build complex yet ruthlessly optimized networks of sourcing, producing, and transporting all of the things we consume on a daily basis right to our doorstep. I decided then and there that I wanted to learn more, so I moved to Bordeaux, France to pursue a second master's degree in Global Supply Chain Management



Choosing to pursue a second master's degree was one of the most rewarding decisions I've made in my life! Fast forward two years later and I have graduated and am working full-time as a Demand Planning Analyst, working at Kellogg's in the IBP team of the Benelux region. The year has been crucial to my development. Not only have I learned an immense deal about the food industry, I've had the opportunity to grow in areas such as Business Forecasting, Integrated Business Planning, financial budget reconciliation, production planning, and data analytics. My career objective is to keep developing myself while cultivating an international, mult-cultural mindset.
